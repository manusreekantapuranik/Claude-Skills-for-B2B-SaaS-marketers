# Landing Page Copy

## What is a Landing Page Copy Skill?
Complete copy for a B2B SaaS landing page covering every section from hero 
to footer. Written to convert a specific ICP. Outcome focused. Jargon free.
Saved as: outputs/landing-page-copy.docx

## How

### Step 1 - Load context
Always read:
Read icp.md
Read brand.md
Read voice.md

Read if available:
If outputs/jtbd-analysis.docx exists 
read it.
If outputs/positioning.docx exists 
read it.
If outputs/messaging-framework.docx 
exists read it.

If none of the outputs exist ask:
What is the product?
Who is the ICP?
What is the one outcome the product 
delivers?
What is the primary conversion goal 
of this page?

### Step 2 - Define the page goal
Before writing a single word establish:

- What is the one action this page must drive?
- Who is the primary visitor?
- Where are they coming from?
- What do they already know?
- What objection will they arrive with?

The entire page is built around one conversion goal. One CTA. Nothing else.

### Step 3 - Write the hero section
The hero must pass the 5 second test. A first time visitor must understand what the product does, who it is for, and why it matters within 5 seconds.

Hero headline:
- Under 8 words
- Outcome focused not feature focused
- Addresses the core pain point
- No jargon
- No clever wordplay that obscures 
  meaning

Hero subheadline:
- 1 to 2 sentences
- Expands on the headline
- Names the ICP explicitly
- States the functional job
- Connects to the emotional outcome

Primary CTA:
- Action verb plus benefit
- Not a generic label
- Reflects what the visitor gets
- Placed above the fold
- Single CTA only

Trust signal line:
- One line below the CTA
- Removes the biggest objection
- No credit card required, 
  free trial, used by X companies

### Step 4 - Write the social proof bar
Immediately below the hero.
Logo bar of recognisable customers.
One line that frames the logos.

### Step 5 - Write the problem section
Name the pain the ICP lives with daily.
Make them feel understood before selling anything.

Cover:
- The specific situation they are in
- What is failing them right now
- The cost of that failure
- Why existing solutions fall short

3 to 5 short punchy statements. No fluff.

### Step 6 - Write the solution section
Introduce the product as the answer 
to the problem just named.

Cover:
- What the product does in one sentence
- How it works at a high level
- What changes for the buyer after 
  they start using it

Do not list features here.
Show transformation. Before and after.

### Step 7 - Write the features and benefits section
Translate every feature into an outcome.

For each feature:
- Feature name as a short header
- One sentence on what it does
- One sentence on what that means 
  for the buyer

3 to 5 features maximum.
Lead with the most important one.
Never list a feature without its benefit.

### Step 8 - Write the social proof section
Place testimonials near the CTA.
This is objection removal not decoration.

For each testimonial include:
- A specific outcome or result
- The person's name and title
- Their company name

2 to 3 testimonials minimum.
Avoid vague quotes.
Specific beats generic every time.

### Step 9 - Write the objection handling section
Answer the top 3 objections the ICP arrives with.
Format as FAQ.

Common B2B SaaS objections:
- How long does it take to set up
- Will it work with our existing tools
- What happens to our data
- Is it worth the price
- What if we need to cancel

Answer each one directly and briefly.

### Step 10 - Write the closing CTA section
Repeat the core value proposition.
One final push to convert.

Include:
- Restatement of the outcome 
  the buyer gets
- Primary CTA repeated
- Trust signal line repeated
- Urgency or scarcity only 
  if genuine

### Step 11 - Review against these rules
Before saving check every section:

- Hero headline is under 8 words.
- There is only one CTA on the page.
- Every feature has a benefit attached.
- Social proof is near the CTA.
- No section uses jargon.
- The ICP is named explicitly.

The page answers these four questions:
What is this. Is it for me. What can I do with it. How is it different.
Copy speaks to the buyer not about the product.
Hero passes the 5 second test.
