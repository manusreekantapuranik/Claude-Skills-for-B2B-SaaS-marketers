# JTBD Analysis

## What is a JTBD analysis skill?
A structured Jobs To Be Done analysis that identifies the functional, emotional 
and social jobs a B2B SaaS ICP is trying to accomplish and the trigger that causes 
them to start looking for a solution. Output informs positioning, messaging, 
and all campaign copy.
Saved as: outputs/jtbd-analysis.docx

## How does JTBD analysis work?

### Step 1 - Load context
Read icp.md
Read brand.md
Read voice.md

### Step 2 - Define the job executor
Identify who is doing the job.

In B2B SaaS there are three roles:

Job Executor
The person using the product daily.

Buyer
The person making the financial decision.

Influencer
The person who shapes the decision without owning it.

For each role define:
- Title and seniority
- What they are responsible for
- What success looks like for them
- What failure looks like for them

### Step 3 - Define the core functional job
The practical task the ICP is trying to accomplish.

Write it as: verb + object + context

The functional job must be:
- Stable even when products change
- Solution agnostic
- Specific not abstract

### Step 4 - Define emotional and social jobs
Emotional job: How does the executor want to FEEL when the job is done?

Format: Feel [state] when [situation]

Social job: How do they want to be PERCEIVED by others?

Format: Be seen as [perception] by [audience]

### Step 5 - Map desired outcomes
For the core functional job identify 
3 to 5 desired outcome statements.

Format:
Minimize the time it takes to 
[functional job step]

or

Increase the likelihood of 
[desired result]

These become proof points in messaging.

### Step 6 - Identify the trigger event
What specific event causes the ICP to 
start looking for a solution?

This is not a demographic. It is a moment.

The trigger drives campaign hooks, ad copy, and email subject lines.

### Step 7 - Identify what they switch from
What solution is currently failing them?

This could be:
- A direct competitor
- A spreadsheet or manual process
- Doing nothing
- An internal tool

This reveals the real competitive set and the strongest differentiation angle.

### Step 8 - Write the JTBD statement
Combine all findings into one statement.

Format:
When [trigger event], [job executor] 
wants to [functional job] so they can 
[emotional job] and be seen as 
[social job] by [audience].

### Step 9 - Save output
Save as: outputs/jtbd-analysis.docx

## When
- Always run this skill first before positioning, messaging, campaign brief, or any copy
- When asked about buyer motivation
- When asked why customers buy
- When asked about ICP pain points
